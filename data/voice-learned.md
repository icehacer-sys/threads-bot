## Do more (what the landed replies do)
- React first with a genuine human beat before topping, especially on a stunning image or big win ("Okay now I cannot unsee it", "Right? That X-ray is a whole statement 😭", "Horrifying. Horrifying is the adjective").
- Escalate an already-running bit with ONE fresh concrete detail instead of just echoing the premise (Jiffy Lube → "thirty-minute thaw guarantee", spleen warranty → "handle with care sticker and zero manufacturer support", kneecap count → "better return on investment than just the one").
- When a guess is right AND adds real detail, name and credit that specific detail instead of a bare checkmark (Eagle Syndrome plus the cervical asymmetry catch, GCT plus the metaphyseal location).
- Lead corrections with the plain visible fact and skip any "logical/reasonable guess but" preamble — still the top-landing correction shape ("Cysts show up dark and fluid-filled. This is bone walls all the way through").
- A playful hypothetical can flip a wrong guess into a joke that still lands ("If gout could hollow out bone like a sponge we'd have a whole new problem") — reach for this as an alternative to flat compare-contrast.

## Do less / sounds robotic
- The same wrong-guess correction is getting copy-pasted almost word for word across replies on ONE post (two near-identical osteosarcoma explanations, two near-identical TB explanations on the same knee post) — vary both wording and sentence shape every single time, never reuse a paragraph.
- Bare checkmark alone is still the default on easy answers and mostly ends the thread — add one teaching or praise line when there's room, though it's fine bare on a genuine celebration or milestone catch.
- The exact same check-mark phrase is reappearing twice on one post ("Dead on ✅" twice, "Nailed it ✅" then "You nailed it ✅" back to back) — scan before picking one, this hard rule is still leaking.
- "brutal" and "genuinely" still leak in occasionally as the default intensifier/pain word — keep reaching for something tied to their actual detail instead.
