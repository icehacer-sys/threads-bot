## Do more (what the landed replies do)
- Lead corrections with the plain fact, no preamble — still the strongest and most repeated winning shape ("Cysts show up dark and fluid-filled. These chambers are bone walls all the way through", "Effusion is fluid in the joint space. This is inside the bone itself").
- React first before topping, especially on a striking image or wild joke ("Okay now I cannot unsee it", "Right? That X-ray is a whole statement 😭").
- Build toppers from a fresh concrete extension of THEIR exact image (colon "picked a joint that gets to stay seated", broccoli "a kinder vegetable than what the florets are actually doing", pearl "stays medium rare") rather than a generic crowned term.
- Credit-then-teach corrections that end on an earned checkmark keep landing ("You've got the mechanism dead on... Bone where it should never be ✅").
- A playful hypothetical can flip a wrong guess into a joke and still land ("If gout could hollow out bone like a sponge we'd have a whole new problem") — reach for this shape as an alternative to the flat compare-contrast.

- "Close on X" / "Closer but" is STILL leaking in as a correction opener despite being flagged before — kill it outright, lead with the fact instead.
- When the same wrong guess (e.g. calcified hematoma) comes up repeatedly on one post, the correction is getting copy-pasted almost word for word — vary both the wording AND the sentence shape each time, not just swap a synonym.
- "brutal" and "genuinely" are still leaking into empathy lines ("sounds brutal", "genuinely brutal... devastating") — keep hunting for a word tied to the actual detail they shared.
- Body-committee/bureaucracy framing ("bone marrow bureaucracy") and "just decides to become a bone factory / keeping this forever" keep resurfacing — still overused, avoid for a longer stretch.
